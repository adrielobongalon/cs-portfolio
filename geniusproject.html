Genius Project

I go to school at a place called Summit Shasta. Every 10th grader has a project called the "Genius Project" where we work on something that interests us. It's sort of based off this thing at Google where their employees get to spend 20% of their time working on whatever project that interests them. I'm not entirely sure if that's true or not, but either way, at our school, we get to work on something of our choice. On of our assignments is to create a blog. So here it is.

Entry 1

So I've been thinking…

So something I like, huh? Well, to be honest, I had a lot of time to think about what I want to do for this project. Even though this project actually started in the middle of the school year, I knew about it pretty early on, since several people (mostly 11th graders I know) told me about it near the start. And I've had a few ideas.

  • make a scarf
  • learn Japanese
  • learn Tagalog
  • code something
  • create a song mashup

Perhaps I should explain a little further. So it's winter, and I've been thinking that I'd like to have a scarf. But I'm a rather picky person when it comes to fashion, and I don't like what I see in stores or online. So I thought I'd make it myself. So why don't I learn to knit one? Oh yeah. I'm too lazy to. But that won't stop me. Instead, I'll create a loom out of wood and 3D-printed material. Still, I don't think I should do this for my Genius Project, since it's basically just following someone else's intructions (I found the 3D models of the parts online, made by someone else, so it's not exactly my project). But I'm still going to make the scarf, since I really want one. It gets cold around here.

I also thought about learning Japanese, since it's the language I feel I understand the most (that is, conversationally; I take Spanish at school, and I can read and write somewhat well, but I am not too good at listening to a conversation and understanding it). I also thought about learning Tagalog. It's the language my parents speak, but for some reason they never taught me. In addition, my friend is doing that for her Genius Project (you can find her blog here), so we could help each other.

Another idea I had was doing some sort of coding project. Perhaps I could expand this site with more coding projects. And as much as I love coding, it kind of feels like cheating my making this my Genius Project, since I already work on it anyways.

So I thought of something new I could try. Something I would enjoy. And I decided I would try to make a mashup. Here are some examples of mashups I've seen before (created by other people, of course; not me).

My first thought was to learn to make an EDM song, because that's the kind of music I like. Perhaps it would have been glitch hop or electro house, but that wouldn't show the "community aspect" of my project. It needs to have an effect on people. And making a song mainly affects me. So I thought of ways to involve others. And then it hit me. I would ask other people for song suggestions, and use them all in a mashup. That way, other people play a part in my project too. And I think I could make people happy by combining their songs together. Although maybe few people might be bothered if their song gets mixed with other songs they don't like. But I'm sure that most people will be fine with it.
